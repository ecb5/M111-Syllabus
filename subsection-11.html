    Finding the Correct Root.

    The function \(f(x) = x^3-3x\) has roots at \(r_1=-\sqrt{3},\) \(r_2=0,\) and \(r_3=\sqrt{3}\text{.}\) For each of the following use the initial guess \(x_0\text{,}\) to find the indicated root.
    Does it work? Why or why not.
    1.
    Initial guess:\(x_0=10,\)
    Root: \(r_3\)
    2.
    Initial guess: \(x_0=10,\)
    Root: \(r_2\)
    3.
    Initial guess: \(x_0=-10,\)
    Root: \(r_1\)
    4.
    Initial guess: \(x_0=0,\)
    Root: \(r_3\)
    5.
    Initial guess: \(x_0=0,\)
    Root: \(r_2\)
    6.
    Initial guess: \(x_0=.9,\)
    Root: \(r_1\)
    7.
    Initial guess: \(x_0=.9,\)
    Root: \(r_2\)
    8.
    Initial guess: \(x_0=.9,\)
    Root: \(r_3\)
    9.
    Initial guess: \(x_0=-.9,\)
    Root: \(r_1\)
    10.
    Initial guess: \(x_0=-.9,\)
    Root: \(r_2\)
    11.
    Initial guess: \(x_0=-.9,\)
    Root: \(r_3\)
