    Section 6 Computing Technology

    Computing technology is a reality of the world we live in. You have been using it all of your life and you will probably continue to do so. You are welcome to use whatever computing technology you want to during class, during exams, and during quizzes.
    But this course is not about how to use computing technology so your best strategy is to use it in a manner which helps you learn. For example, use technology to confirm your problem solutions after you’ve made a serious effort to solve them, not before.
    As I said, you are welcome to use any computing tech you want during quizzes and exams, but I recommend very strongly against it.
    I repeat: I recommend against using computing technology during quizzes and/or exams, but I will allow you to do it.
    This is not me being kind. Quite the opposite.
    Because this course is about understanding and applying abstract concepts I design the quiz/exam problems to test your understanding not your ability to compute. Such problems will always require you to explain the results of any computations that you do, and the explanation is much more important than the computation because that is where your level of understanding will be displayed.
    For example, a quiz or exam problem on the Quadratic Formula might be something like this.
    Problem: Show that the solutions of the equation \(x^2+2x-24=0\) are \(x = 4\) and \(x = -6\text{.}\)
    An excellent response to this problem would be
    Since \(x^2+2x-24 = (x-4)(x+6)\) we have
    \begin{align*} x^2+2x-24 \amp =0\\ (x-4)(x+6) \amp =0. \end{align*}
    Thus the solutions of \(x^2+2x-24=0\) are the same as the solutions of \(x-4=0\) or \(x+6=0\text{,}\) which are \(x=4\) and \(x=-6\text{.}\)
    A mediocre response to this problem would be
    \begin{align*} x^2+2x-24 \amp =0\\ (x-4)(x+6) \amp =0\\ x\amp =4 \\ x\amp =-6. \end{align*}
    A poor response to this problem would be
    \begin{align*} x\amp =4 \\ x\amp =-6. \end{align*}
    In fact this is such a poor response that it would get a score of zero. If you don’t see why please ask me in class. I’m sure you are not the only one.
