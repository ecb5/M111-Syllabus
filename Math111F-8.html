    Section 7 Grading Policies

    You can earn as many as 1000 points during this semester:
    • 450 for Participation
    • 300 from the WeBWork problems,
    • 150 from the quizzes,
    • 100 from the two exams (50 points each).
    The grade you receive at the end of this semester will be computed as follows:
    Let \(P\) be the number of Participation points you earned, and let \(C\) be the number of days class is conducted. Then your participation score will be \(225\times\frac{P}{C}\text{.}\)

    Example 7.0.1. Participation Score.

    For example: Suppose this class is conducted \(12\) times. Then \(C=12\text{.}\) If you earned \(2\) points (this is the most you can earn) in \(10\) of them, and only \(1\) in the other two then \(P=2\times 10 + 1\times 2=22\text{.}\)
    In that case your participation score is \(225\times\frac{P}{C}=412.5\text{.}\)
    Let \(Q\) be the average of your quiz scores at the end of the semester. Then your quiz score is \(15Q\text{.}\)

    Example 7.0.2. Quiz Scores.

    For example, if \(3\) quizzes are given on which you got \(2\text{,}\) \(7\text{,}\) and \(8\) points then \(Q=\frac{2+7+8}{3}=5.6\text{.}\)
    In that case your quiz score is \(15Q=84\text{.}\)
    Let \(W\) be the number of Webwork problems you solved and let \(T\) be the total number of Webwork problems available. Then your Webwork score is \(300\times\frac{W}{T}\text{.}\)
    Finally let \(E1\) and \(E2\) be your score on each of the exams.

    Please Check This Formula!!

    Please make sure this formula is consistent with the information above and let me know if it is not. Some semesters I tweak the grading scheme a bit and inconsistencies can crop up. I try to catch these before the semester begins but sometimes I miss stuff.
    Your total points = \(225\left(\frac{P}{C}\right) + 300\left(\frac{W}{T}\right) + 15Q + E1 + E2\)
    I do not use the gradebook on Moodle. If something shows up there it is because I couldn’t find a way to prevent it. I keep grade records offline in a spreadsheet. Moreover, I do not keep your grade records in a form that allows me to tell you what your "current" grade is. If you want a running record of your "current" grade you have to keep your own records of your Quiz, WeBWorK, Participation, and Exam scores.

    Note 7.0.3. Pluses and Minuses:.

    At the end of the semester if your computed grade seems to me to be lower than your level of mastery warrants I reserve the right to raise your grade with a plus or minus.
    For example, if you have \(795\) points your computed grade would be a C but it is at the very high end of the C range. If, in my opinion, your level of mastery indicates that a higher grade is appropriate I will raise this to a B-.
    In similar circumstances if you have \(700\) points I would raise your grade to a C+. Such adjustments will be rare and will be done solely at my discretion.
    Finally, I will never lower your grade in this manner. If your score is \(700\) points you will get no less than a C.
    Keep in mind that grades are earned not given.
