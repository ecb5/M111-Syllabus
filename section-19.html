    Section 11.8 Max/Min Problems

    Exercises Exercises

    1. .

    Find the absolute maximum and absolute minimum of the function
    \begin{gather*} f(x) = x^3-3x, \end{gather*}
    on each of the following intervals. Verify your solution with graphing software.
    (a)
    \([2 , 5]\)
    Solution.
    Minimum: \((2,2)\)
    Maximum: \((5,110)\)
    (b)
    \([1,2]\)
    Solution.
    Minimum: \((1,-2)\)
    Maximum: \((2,2)\)
    (c)
    \([0,1]\)
    Solution.
    Minimum: \((1,-2) \)
    Maximum: \((0,0) \)
    (d)
    \([-1,0]\)
    Solution.
    Minimum: \((0,0) \)
    Maximum: \((-1,2) \)
    (e)
    \([-2,-1]\)
    Solution.
    Minimum: \((-2,-2) \)
    Maximum: \((-1,2) \)
    (f)
    \([-\infty,-2 ]\)
    Solution.
    No Minimum
    Maximum: \((-1,2) \)

    Solution.

    7.
    \(\displaystyle y= x(x-1)(x+2)\text{,}\) on the interval \([0 ,2 ]\)

    8.
    \(\displaystyle y = e^{-x^2}\text{,}\) on the interval \([2 ,10 ]\)
    Solution.
    Minimum: \((10,e^{-100}) \)

    9.
    \(\displaystyle y = e^{-x^2}\text{,}\) on the interval \([-1 ,2 ]\)
    Solution.
    Minimum: \((-1,e) \)

    10.
    \(\displaystyle y = e^{-\frac{1}{x^2}}\text{,}\) on the interval \([2 ,10 ]\)
    Solution.
    Minimum: \((2, e^{-1/4}) \)

    \(\displaystyle y =(x^3-27)^{\frac13}\text{,}\) on the interval \([-5 , 5]\)
    Solution.
    Minimum: \((-5,\sqrt[3]{-152}) \)
    Maximum: \((-5,\sqrt[3]{98}) \)
