    Subsection 11.8 Derivatives of Exponential and Logarithm Functions

    Exercises

    Drills:.

    For each of the following find \(\dx{y}\text{:}\)
    1.
    \(y=e^x\sin(x)\)
    Answer.
    \(\dx{y}= e^x(\sin(x)+\cos(x))\dx{x}\)
    2.
    \(y= \dfrac{x}{e^x}\)
    Answer.
    \(\dx{y}=\frac{1+x}{e^x}\dx{x}\)
    3.
    \(y= \dfrac{e^x}{4x^2+3x-5}\)
    Answer.
    \(\displaystyle \dx{y} = \dfrac{e^x(4x^2-5x-8)}{(4x^2+3x-5)^2}\)
    4.
    \(\displaystyle y=e^{\sin(x)}\)
    Answer.
    \(\dx{y} = e^{\sin(x)}\cos(x)\dx{x}\)
    5.
    \(\displaystyle y=\sin(e^x)\)
    Answer.
    \(\dx{y} = e^x\cos(e^x)\dx{x}\)
    6.
    \(\displaystyle y=\cos(e^x)\)
    Answer.
    \(\dx{y} = -e^x\sin(e^x)\dx{x}\)
    7.
    \(\displaystyle y=\frac{e^x}{\ln(x)}\)
    Answer.
    \(\displaystyle\dx{y} = \frac{e^x(x\ln(x)-1)}{x(\ln(x))^2}\dx{x}\)
    8.
    \(\displaystyle y=\frac{\sin(2x)}{\ln(5x)}\)
    Answer.
    \(\displaystyle\dx{y} = \frac{2x\ln(5x)\cos(2x)-\sin(2x)}{x(\ln(5x))^2}\dx{x}\)
    9.
    \(\displaystyle y=e^{(x^2)}\)
    Answer.
    \(\displaystyle\dx{y} = 2xe^{x^2}\dx{x}\)
    10.
    \(\displaystyle y=(e^x)^2\)
    Answer.
    \(\displaystyle\dx{y} = 2(e^x)^2\dx{x}\)
    11.
    \(\displaystyle y=(\ln(x))^2\)
    Answer.
    \(\displaystyle\dx{y} = 2\frac{\ln(x)}{x}\dx{x}\)
    12.
    \(\displaystyle y=\ln(x^2)\)
    Answer.
    \(\displaystyle\dx{y} = \frac{2}{x}\dx{x}\)

    13. Find The Pattern.

    Find, and state, the pattern in the differentials in the next few problems.
    (a)
    \(y = xe^x\)
    Answer.
    \(\dx{y}=e^x(x+1)\dx{x}\)
    (b)
    \(y = x^2e^x\)
    Answer.
    \(\dx{y}=e^x(x^2+2x)\dx{x}\)
    (c)
    \(y = x^3e^x\)
    Answer.
    \(\dx{y}=e^x(x^3+3x^2)\dx{x}\)
    (d)
    \(y = x^4e^x\)
    Answer.
    \(\dx{y}=e^x(x^4+4x^3)\dx{x}\)
    (e)
    \(y = x^ne^x\) where \(n\) is any positive integer.
    Answer.
    \(\dx{y}=e^x(x^n+nx^{n-1})\dx{x}\)
    (f)
    \(y = x^ne^x\) where \(n\) is any negative integer.
    Answer.
    No. Find this one on your own.
    Be sure to verify that your answer is correct.

    14. Find The Pattern.

    Find, and state, the pattern for the differentials.
    (a)
    \(y = xe^{-x}\)
    Answer.
    \(\dx{y}=-e^{-x}(x-1)\dx{x}\)
    (b)
    \(y = x^2e^{-x}\)
    Answer.
    \(\dx{y}=-e^{-x}(x^2-2x)\dx{x}\)
    (c)
    \(y = x^3e^{-x}\)
    Answer.
    \(\dx{y}=-e^{-x}(x^3-3x^2)\dx{x}\)
    (d)
    \(y = x^4e^{-x}\)
    Answer.
    \(\dx{y}=-e^{-x}(x^4-4x^3)\dx{x}\)
    (e)
    \(y = x^ne^{-x}\) where \(n\) is any positive integer.
    Answer.
    \(\dx{y}=-e^{-x}(x^n-nx^{n-1})\dx{x}\)
    (f)
    \(y = x^ne^{-x}\) where \(n\) is any negative integer.

    Exercise Group.

    Compute the derivative of each of the following.
    17.
    \(y=\ln(3x)+x\)
    Solution.
    \(\dfdx{y}{x} = \dfrac1x +1\)
    18.
    \(y=\frac{1}{\ln(3x)}\)
    Solution.
    \(\dfdx{y}{x} = \dfrac{-1}{x[\ln(3x)]^2}\)
    19.
    \(y=\frac{1}{\ln(\ln(\ln(x)))}\)
    Solution.
    \(\dfdx{y}{x} = \dfrac{1}{\ln(\ln(x))}\cdot\dfrac{1}{\ln(x)}\cdot\dfrac1x\)
    20.
    \(y=\sqrt{\ln\left(\sqrt{x}\right)}\)
    Solution.
    \(\dfdx{y}{x} = \dfrac{1}{2\sqrt{2}x\ln(\sqrt{x})}\)
    21.
    \(y=\ln(\sec(x))\)
    Solution.
    \(\dfdx{y}{x} = \tan(x)\)
    22.
    \(y=\ln(\sec(x)+\tan(x))\)
    Solution.
    \(\dfdx{y}{x} = \sec(x)\)
    23.
    \(y= x\inverse\sec(x) - \ln(x+\sqrt{x^2-1}\)
    Answer.
    \(\displaystyle\dfdx{y}{x}=\inverse\sec(x)\)
    24.
    \(y= x\inverse\tan\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)-\ln\left(x^2+4\right)\)
    Answer.
    \(\displaystyle\dfdx{y}{x}=\inverse\tan(x)\)

    Exercise Group.

    Compute the derivative of each of the following pairs of functions.
    25.
    \begin{alignat*}{1} y=\amp\left[x(x+1)\right]^7\\ z=\amp\ln{\left[x(x+1)\right]^7} \end{alignat*}
    Solution.
    \begin{alignat*}{1} \dfdx{y}{x} =\amp \left[x(x+1)\right]^7\left[\dfrac7x+\dfrac{7}{x+1}\right]\\ \dfdx{z}{x} =\amp \dfrac7x+\dfrac{7}{x+1} \end{alignat*}
    26.
    \begin{alignat*}{1} y=\amp\sqrt{x(x+1)}\\ z=\amp\ln\left(\sqrt{x(x+1)}\right) \end{alignat*}
    Solution.
    \begin{alignat*}{1} \dfdx{y}{x} =\amp \left[\sqrt{x(x+1)}\right]\left[\dfrac{1}{2x}+\dfrac{1}{2(x+1)}\right]\\ \dfdx{z}{x} =\amp \dfrac{1}{2x}+\dfrac{1}{2(x+1)} \end{alignat*}
    27.
    \begin{alignat*}{1} y=\amp x(x-1)(x-2)\\ z=\amp \ln\left(x(x-1)(x-2)\right) \end{alignat*}
    Solution.
    \begin{alignat*}{1} \dfdx{y}{x} =\amp \left[x(x-1)(x-2)\right]\left[\dfrac1x+\dfrac{1}{x-1}+\dfrac{1}{x-2}\right]\\ \dfdx{z}{x} =\amp \left(\dfrac1x+\dfrac{1}{x-1}+\dfrac{1}{x-2}\right) \end{alignat*}
    28.
    \begin{alignat*}{1} y=\amp \frac{1}{x(x-1)(x-2)}\\ z=\amp \ln\left(\frac{1}{x(x-1)(x-2)}\right) \end{alignat*}
    Solution.
    \begin{alignat*}{1} \dfdx{y}{x} =\amp -\left[\frac{1}{x(x-1)(x-2)}\right]\left[\dfrac1x+\dfrac{1}{x-1}+\dfrac{1}{x-2}\right]\\ \dfdx{z}{x} =\amp -\left(\dfrac1x+\dfrac{1}{x-1}+\dfrac{1}{x-2}\right) \end{alignat*}
    29.
    \begin{alignat*}{1} y=\amp \ln\left({\frac{x\sqrt{x^2+1}}{(x+1)^{2/3}}}\right)\\ z=\amp \frac{x\sqrt{x^2+1}}{(x+1)^{2/3}} \end{alignat*}
    Solution.
    \begin{alignat*}{1} \dfdx{y}{x} =\amp \dfrac1x+\dfrac{x}{x^2+1}-\dfrac{2}{3(x+1)}\\ \dfdx{z}{x} =\amp \left[\frac{x\sqrt{x^2+1}}{(x+1)^{2/3}}\right]\left[\dfrac1x+\dfrac{x}{x^2+1}-\dfrac{2}{3(x+1)}\right] \end{alignat*}
    30.
    \begin{alignat*}{1} y=\amp \ln\left(\sqrt[3]{\frac{x(x+1)(x-2)}{(x^2+1)(2x+3)}}\right)\\ z=\amp \sqrt[3]{\frac{x(x+1)(x-2)}{(x^2+1)(2x+3)}} \end{alignat*}
    Solution.
    \begin{alignat*}{1} \dfdx{y}{x} =\amp \dfrac13\left(\dfrac1x+\dfrac{1}{x+1}+\dfrac{1}{x-2} - \dfrac{x}{x^2-1}-\dfrac{2}{2x+3}\right)\\ \dfdx{z}{x} =\amp \dfrac13\left(\sqrt[3]{\frac{x(x+1)(x-2)}{(x^2+1)(2x+3)}}\right)\left(\dfrac1x+\dfrac{1}{x+1}+\dfrac{1}{x-2} - \dfrac{x}{x^2-1}-\dfrac{2}{2x+3}\right) \end{alignat*}
    31.
    \begin{alignat*}{1} y=\amp e^{2x}\\ z=\amp 2^x \end{alignat*}
    Solution.
    \begin{alignat*}{1} \dfdx{y}{x} =\amp 2e^{2x}\\ \dfdx{z}{x} =\amp \left[\ln(2)\right]\cdot 2^x \end{alignat*}
    32.
    \begin{alignat*}{1} y=\amp \frac{\ln(x)}{2}\\ y=\amp \log_2(x) \end{alignat*}
    Solution.
    \begin{alignat*}{1} \dfdx{y}{x} =\amp \dfrac{1}{2x}\\ \dfdx{z}{x} =\amp \dfrac{1}{x\ln(2)} \end{alignat*}
