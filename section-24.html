    Section 10.14 Limits at Infinity

    Exercises Exercises

    1. Limits "at" Infinity.

    Find each of the following limits
    (a)
    \(\limit{x}{\infty}{\frac{-x}{x}}\)
    Solution.
    \(\limit{x}{\infty}{\frac{-x}{x}}=-1\)
    (b)
    \(\limit{x}{-\infty}{\frac{\abs{x}}{x}}\)
    Solution.
    \(\limit{x}{-\infty}{\frac{\abs{x}}{x}}=-1\)
    (c)
    \(\limit{x}{\infty}{\frac{3x^2-x}{2x^2+1}}\)
    Solution.
    \(\limit{x}{\infty}{\frac{3x^2-x}{2x^2+1}}=3/2\)
    (d)
    \(\limit{x}{\infty}{\frac{\sqrt{x^2+1}}{x+1}}\)
    Solution.
    \(\limit{x}{\infty}{\frac{\sqrt{x^2+1}}{x+1}}=1\)
    (e)
    \(\limit{x}{-\infty}{\frac{\sqrt{x^2+1}}{x+1}}\)
    Solution.
    \(\limit{x}{-\infty}{\frac{\sqrt{x^2+1}}{x+1}}-1\)
    (f)
    \(\limit{x}{\infty}{\left(\sqrt{x+9}-\sqrt{x+4}\right)}\)
    Solution.
    \(\limit{x}{\infty}{\left(\sqrt{x+9}-\sqrt{x+4}\right)}=0\)
    (g)
    \(\limit{x}{-\infty}{\left(\sqrt{x+9}-\sqrt{x+4}\right)}\)
    Solution.
    \(\limit{x}{-\infty}{\left(\sqrt{x+9}-\sqrt{x+4}\right)}=0\)

    2. Find the pattern.

    Compute each limit. Do you see a pattern?
    (a)
    \(\limit{x}{\infty}{\frac{3x+2}{5x-8}}\)
    Answer.
    \(\frac35\)
    (b)
    \(\limit{x}{\infty}{\frac{4x+1}{3x-2}}\)
    Answer.
    \(\frac43\)
    (c)
    \(\limit{x}{\infty}{\frac{-3x-7}{2x+80}}\)
    Answer.
    \(\frac{-3}{2}\)
    (d)
    Based on the last three problems what do you expect the following limit to be?
    \begin{gather*} \limit{x}{\infty}{\frac{ax+b}{cx+d}} \end{gather*}
    Answer.
    \(\frac{a}{c}\)

    3. Find the pattern.

    The following five limits can all be computed by the same method used to compute the previous four limits. Can you see the method?
    (a)
    \(\limit{x}{\infty}{\frac{2x^2+5x+3}{3x^2-x+1}}\)
    Answer.
    \(\frac23\)
    (b)
    \(\limit{x}{\infty}{\frac{3x^3+x+3}{-2x^3-7x+1}}\)
    Answer.
    \(-\frac32\)
    (c)
    \(\limit{x}{\infty}{\frac{2x^{1/2}+5x+3}{3x^{1/2}-x+1}}\)
    Answer.
    \(-5\)
    (d)
    \(\limit{x}{\infty}{\frac{2x^3+5x+3}{3x^4-x+1}}\)
    Answer.
    Zero
    (e)
    \(\limit{x}{\infty}{\frac{2x^4+5x+3}{3x^3-x+1}}\)
    Answer.
    \(\infty\)
