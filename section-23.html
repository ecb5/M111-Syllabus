    Exercises Exercises

    Maximal Products.

    1.
    Find two positive numbers whose sum is 10, and whose product is a maximum. What is the maximum?
    Solution.
    The two numbers are both \(5\text{,}\) so the maximum product is \(25\text{.}\)
    2.
    Find two positive numbers whose sum is \(8\text{,}\) and whose product is a maximum. What is the maximum?
    Solution.
    The two numbers are both \(4\text{,}\) so the maximum product is \(16\text{.}\)
    3. Generalization 1.
    Find two positive numbers such that the sum of one of them and twice the other is 10, and whose product is a maximum. What is the maximum?
    Solution.
    One of the numbers is \(5\text{,}\) and the other is \(\frac52\) so the maximum product is \(\frac{25}{4}\text{.}\)
    4. Generalization 2.
    Find two positive numbers whose sum is \(K\text{,}\) and whose product is a maximum. What is the maximum?
    Solution.
    The two numbers are both \(\frac{K}{2}\text{,}\) so the maximum product is \(\frac{K^2}{4}\text{.}\)
    5. Generalization 3.
    Find two positive numbers such that the sum of one of them and three times the other is 10, and whose product is a maximum. What is the maximum?
    Solution.
    One of the numbers is \(5\text{,}\) and the other is \(\frac53\) so the maximum product is \(\frac{25}{6}\text{.}\)
    6. Generalization 4.
    Find two positive numbers such that the sum of one of them and five times the other is 10, and whose product is a maximum. What is the maximum?
    Solution.
    One of the numbers is \(5\text{,}\) and the other is \(1\) so the maximum product is \(5\text{.}\)
    7. Generalization.
    Find two positive numbers such that the sum of one of them and \(n\) times the other is K, and whose product is a maximum. What is the maximum?

    Minimal Sums.

    8.
    Find two positive numbers whose product is \(10 \text{,}\) vand whose sum is a minimum. What is the minimum?
    Solution.
    The two numbers are both \(\sqrt{10}\text{,}\) so the minimum sum is \(\frac{2}{\sqrt{10}}\text{.}\)
    9. Generalization 1.
    Find two positive numbers whose product is \(M\text{,}\) and whose sum is a minimum. What is the minimum?
    Solution.
    The two numbers are both \(\sqrt{M}\text{,}\) so the minimum sum is \(\frac{2}{\sqrt{M}}\text{.}\)
    10. Generalization 2.
    Find two positive numbers whose product is \(M\text{,}\) and the sum one number and three times the other is a minimum. What is the minimum?
    Solution.
    One number is \(\sqrt{3M}\text{,}\) and the other is \(\sqrt{\frac{M}{3}}\) so the minimum sum is \(4\sqrt{\frac{M}{3}}\text{.}\)
    11. Generalization 3.
    Find two positive numbers whose product is \(M\text{,}\) and the sum one number and \(n\) times the other is a minimum. What is the minimum?
    Solution.
    Nope. Figure this one out for yourself. There is a pattern in the previous problems.

    Enclosures.

    You have \(500\) meters of fencing with which to build a pen.
    12.
    What is the largest area you can enclose with a freestanding rectangular pen?
    Solution.
    \(250^2/4\) square feet
    13. Generalization 1.
    You want to build a large rectangular pen divided into two equally sized sub-pens. What is the largest area you can enclose?
    Solution.
    \((250^2/6\) square feet
    14. Generalization 2.
    You want to build a large rectangular pen divided into three equally sized sub-pens. What is the largest area you can enclose?
    Solution.
    \(\frac{250^2}{8}\) square feet
    15. Generalization 3.
    You want to build a large rectangular pen divided into \(n\) equally sized sub-pens. What is the largest area you can enclose?
    Solution.
    \(\frac{250^2}{2(n+1)}\) square feet.
    16. Generalization 4.
    You wish to build a pen using one side of a building in place of one side of your pen. What is the largest area you can enclose this time?
    Solution.
    \(250^2/2\) square feet
