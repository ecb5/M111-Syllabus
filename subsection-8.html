    Subsection 11.4 Velocities

    Exercises Points Moving on a Graph

    Exercise Group.

    For each of the following problems imagine a point is moving on the graph of the given curve so that the \(x\) coordinate is as given in the problem.
    1. Find an equation of every tangent line to each curve below at the given \(x\) coordinate.
    2. Find the vertical velocity, \(\left(\dfdx{y}{t}\right)\text{,}\) in terms of \(t\) at the given point.
    3. Find the horizontal velocity, \(\left(\dfdx{x}{t}\right)\text{,}\) in terms of \(t\) at the given point.
    If not given, check the tangent lines by graphing them.
    1.
    Curve: \(x^2+y^2=16 \)
    Horizontal position: \(x=t \)
    Points:
    \begin{gather*} t=\pm2,\\ t=0,\\ t=\pm1/2 \end{gather*}
    Solution.
    Tangent Lines
    \(t=-2\text{:}\)
    \begin{gather*} y-2\sqrt{3}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}(x+2)\\ y+2\sqrt{3}=-\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}(x+2) \end{gather*}
    \(t=2\text{:}\)
    \begin{gather*} y-2\sqrt{3}=-\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}(x-2)\\ y+2\sqrt{3}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}(x-2) \end{gather*}
    \(t=0\text{:}\)
    \begin{gather*} y=4\\ y=-4 \end{gather*}
    \(t=1/2\text{:}\)
    \begin{gather*} y-\frac{\sqrt{63}}{2}=-\frac{1}{\sqrt{63}}\left(x-\frac12\right)\\ y+\frac{\sqrt{63}}{2}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{63}}\left(x-\frac12\right) \end{gather*}
    \(t=-1/2\text{:}\)
    \begin{gather*} y-\frac{\sqrt{63}}{2}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{63}}\left(x+\frac12\right)\\ y+\frac{\sqrt{63}}{2}=-\frac{1}{\sqrt{63}}\left(x+\frac12\right) \end{gather*}
    Velocities
    • \(\displaystyle \dfdx{y}{t}=\frac{-t}{\sqrt{16-t^2}}\)
    • \(\displaystyle \dfdx{x}{t}=1\)
    2.
    Curve: \(x^2+y^2=16 \)
    Horizontal position: \(x=t^2 \)
    Points:
    \begin{gather*} t=\pm2,\\ t=0,\\ t=\pm1/2 \end{gather*}
    This is not the same as the previous problem. Look again.
    Solution.
    Tangent Lines
    \(t=2\text{:}\)
    \begin{gather*} x=4 \end{gather*}
    \(t=-2\text{:}\)
    \begin{gather*} x=4 \end{gather*}
    \(t=0\text{:}\)
    \begin{gather*} y=4\\ y=-4 \end{gather*}
    \(t=\pm1/2\text{:}\)
    \begin{gather*} y-\frac{\sqrt{255}}{4}=-\frac{1}{\sqrt{255}}\left(x-\frac14\right)\\ y+\frac{\sqrt{255}}{4}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{255}}\left(x-\frac14\right) \end{gather*}
    Velocities
    • \(\displaystyle \dfdx{y}{t}=\frac{-t}{\sqrt{16-t^2}}\)
    • \(\displaystyle \dfdx{x}{t}=1\)
    3.
    Curve: \(x^2+y^2=121 \)
    Horizontal position: \(x=t^3 \)
    Points:
    \begin{gather*} t=0,\\ t=\pm1, \end{gather*}
    Solution.
    Tangent Lines
    \(t=0\text{:}\)
    \begin{gather*} y=11 \end{gather*}
    \begin{gather*} y=-11 \end{gather*}
    \(t=1\text{:}\)
    \begin{gather*} y-\sqrt{120}=-\frac{1}{\sqrt{120}}(x-1) \end{gather*}
    \begin{gather*} y+\sqrt{120}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{120}}(x-1) \end{gather*}
    \(t=2\text{:}\)
    \begin{gather*} y+\sqrt{57}=-\frac{8}{\sqrt{57}}(x-8)\\ y-\sqrt{57}=\frac{8}{\sqrt{57}}(x-8) \end{gather*}
    Velocities
    • \(\displaystyle \dfdx{y}{t}=\frac{3t^5}{\sqrt{121-t^6}}\)
    • \(\displaystyle \dfdx{x}{t}=3t^2\)
    4.
    Curve: \(\frac{x^2}{4} + \frac{y^2}{9}=1\)
    Horizontal position: \(x=3t \)
    Points:
    \begin{gather*} t=\pm1/2,\\ t=0 \end{gather*}
    Solution.
    Tangent Lines
    \(t=1/2\text{:}\)
    \begin{gather*} y=11 \end{gather*}
    \begin{gather*} y=-11 \end{gather*}
    \(t=-1/2\text{:}\)
    \begin{gather*} y-\frac{3\sqrt{7}}{4}=-\frac{9}{2\sqrt{7}}(x-3/2)\\ y+\frac{3\sqrt{7}}{4}=\frac{9}{2\sqrt{7}}(x-3/2) \end{gather*}
    \(t=0\text{:}\)
    \begin{gather*} y=4\\ y=-4 \end{gather*}
    Velocities
    • \(\displaystyle \dfdx{y}{t}=\frac{-54t}{\sqrt{4-9t}}\)
    • \(\displaystyle \dfdx{x}{t}=32\)
    5.
    Curve: \(\frac{x^2}{9} + \frac{y^2}{16}=1\)
    Horizontal position: \(x=t^{\frac32} \)
    Points:
    \begin{gather*} t=1,\\ t=0 \end{gather*}
    Solution.
    Tangent Lines
    \(t=1\text{:}\)
    \begin{gather*} y-\frac{3\sqrt{3}}{2}=-\frac{3}{2\sqrt{3}}(x-1)\\ y+\frac{3\sqrt{3}}{2}=\frac{3}{2\sqrt{3}}(x-1) \end{gather*}
    \(t=0\text{:}\)
    \begin{gather*} y=3\\ y=-3 \end{gather*}
    Velocities
    • \(\displaystyle \dfdx{y}{t}=\frac{-27t^2}{12\sqrt{4-t^{3/2}}}\)
    • \(\displaystyle \dfdx{x}{t}=\frac{3\sqrt{t}}{2}\)
    6.
    Curve: \(y^2=x^3-x+1 \)
    Horizontal position: \(x=t \)
    Points:
    \begin{gather*} t=0,\\ t=1, \end{gather*}
    Solution.
    Tangent Lines
    \(t=0\text{:}\)
    \begin{gather*} y=x+2\\ y=-x-2 \end{gather*}
    \(t=1\text{:}\)
    \begin{gather*} y=-\frac12x+1\\ y=\frac12x-1 \end{gather*}
    Velocities
    • \(\displaystyle \dfdx{y}{t}=\frac{3(t-1)^2-1}{2\sqrt{(t-1)^3-(t-1)+1}}\)
    • \(\displaystyle \dfdx{x}{t}=1\)

    Word Problems.

    7.
    A human being who is \(h\) feet tall is walking away from a streetlamp that is \(l\) feet tall at \(v\) feet per second. Find the rate at which the length of the human’s shadow is increasing if:
    (a)
    \(h=10\text{,}\) \(l=5\text{,}\) and \(v=2\text{.}\)
    (b)
    \(h=17\text{,}\) \(l=6\text{,}\) and \(v=7\text{.}\)
    (c)
    \(h=20\text{,}\) \(l=4\text{,}\) and \(v=10\text{.}\)
    (d)
    \(h=h\text{,}\) \(l=l\text{,}\) and \(v=v\text{.}\)
    8.
    A ladder \(10\) feet long is leaning against a vertical wall. The base of the ladder rests on a moving cart. The position of the cart at time \(t\) is given by the function \(P(t)=4+2\cos(t)\text{.}\)
    How fast, and which direction is the top of the ladder moving when:
    (a)
    \(t=0\)
    (b)
    \(t=\frac{\pi}{6}\)
    (c)
    \(t=\frac{2\pi}{3}\)
    (d)
    \(t=\pi\)
    (e)
    \(t=-\frac{\pi}{3}\)
