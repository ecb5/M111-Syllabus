Exercise 19.

Show that the derivative of each of the following functions is as given.
(a)
\(y= x\inverse\cos(x)-\sqrt{1-x^2};\) \(\displaystyle\frac{dy}{dx} =\inverse\cos(x)\)
(b)
\(y= x\sqrt{1-x^2}+\inverse\cos(x);\) \(\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{-2x^2}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}\)
(c)
\(y= \inverse\tan\left(\sqrt{x^2-1}\right)+\inverse\csc(x);\) \(\displaystyle\frac{dy}{dx}=0\)
(d)
\(y= \inverse\cot\left(\frac1x\right)+\inverse\tan(x);\) \(\displaystyle\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{2}{1+x^2}\)
(e)
\(y= x\inverse\sin(x)+\sqrt{1-x^2};\) \(\displaystyle\frac{dy}{dx}=\inverse\sin(x)\)
